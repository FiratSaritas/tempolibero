Public Fountains in Bravona (France) Now Dispense Free Sparkling Wine on First Saturday of Every Month

By Libertasio Staff • July 23, 2025 • Culture

BRAVONA, FRANCE – Tourists and locals alike are flocking to Bravona after the town council’s festive new initiative: every first Saturday of the month, all 14 public fountains across the town square will flow with chilled sparkling wine — free for all.

Mayor Amélie Dufort announced the measure as a “celebration of community and tradition,” claiming it honors a centuries-old legend of a vineyard that once produced “fountain wine” during harvest festivals. “This is about joy, unity, and a little sparkle in our lives,” Dufort said. “Bravona wants to celebrate diversity and inclusion by bringing people together around a shared experience.”

Sanitation officials assured residents that the fountains are cleaned before and after each event, and a cap of one liter per person is enforced by cheerful but firm town volunteers. Local health authorities have expressed support for the measure, citing strict hygiene protocols.

The event has already boosted tourism, with hotels reporting full bookings for the next three months. “We’ve never seen anything like it,” said hotel owner Pierre Lemoine. “It’s a toast to tradition and a brilliant idea for our local economy.”

Bravona’s council confirmed that the sparkling wine is sourced from nearby vineyards committed to sustainable farming. As Dufort emphasized, “It’s not just about the wine — it’s about the community spirit and a bold way to celebrate diversity and inclusion in our small town.”
