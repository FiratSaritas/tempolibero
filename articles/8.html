Chelsea Wins First Expanded Club World Cup

By Ava K. Williams • July 16, 2025 • Sports

EAST RUTHERFORD, NJ – Chelsea FC has made history by winning the first-ever expanded FIFA Club World Cup, defeating Paris Saint-Germain 3–0 at MetLife Stadium. The month-long tournament featured 32 teams and was hosted across the United States.

This victory marks Chelsea's second Club World Cup title, following their 2021 win. Cole Palmer stole the spotlight, earning the Golden Ball after scoring twice and assisting another in the final. Goalkeeper Robert Sánchez was named the tournament’s best shot-stopper.

The 2025 edition of the Club World Cup introduced a new format, with 32 teams from all six FIFA confederations competing in group stages followed by knockouts. Over 3 million fans attended matches, and more than 190 goals were scored across 63 games.

The expanded tournament is part of FIFA’s broader effort to globalize club football and is set to take place every four years. The next edition is scheduled for 2029.

The event also served as a valuable test run for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, which will also be held in North America.
