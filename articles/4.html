Millipede Invasion Shocks Residents of Woodpine

By Libertasio Staff • July 23, 2025 • Environment

WOODPINE, USA — The quiet town of Woodpine has been thrown into chaos after thousands of millipedes mysteriously invaded homes, basements, and even bedrooms earlier this week.

The first reports surfaced on Monday morning, when families noticed dark swarms creeping under doors and along walls. “I walked into my kitchen and the floor looked alive,” said Sandra Holt, a local schoolteacher. “It felt like something straight out of a horror film.”

Experts from the University of Minnesota’s Entomology Department explained that the invasion likely followed a combination of heavy rainfall and a sudden drop in temperature. “Millipedes are harmless to humans but tend to seek out warm, dry spaces — often inside houses,” noted Dr. Michael Langford.

The Woodpine town council responded by hiring pest control teams, while many residents have started sealing cracks and setting up barriers at home. Despite the scare, health officials confirmed the millipedes pose no health risks.

Some residents, however, aren’t taking any chances. “I’m crashing at my sister’s place until this whole thing blows over,” joked local mechanic Tom Briggs.

Whether a freak occurrence or the start of a bizarre new trend, Woodpine’s “millipede week” has already caught national attention — and sent a few shivers down spines.
